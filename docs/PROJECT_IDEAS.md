# Portfolio project directions

The best version for a CV is not “research any topic.” A focused audience and measurable quality
rule make the project easier to explain, test, and demonstrate.

## Ranked recommendations

### 1. AI Research Paper Scout — strongest fit

- **User:** students, junior ML engineers, and research teams.
- **Input:** a research question or arXiv topic.
- **Output:** a comparison of recent papers, methods, datasets, reported metrics, limitations, and
  implementation links.
- **Why it is strong:** it directly supports a GenAI profile and demonstrates tool use, multi-agent
  routing, evidence synthesis, and evaluation.
- **Portfolio metric:** citation validity rate and percentage of extracted metrics verified against
paper text.

### 2. Startup Competitor Intelligence Agent

- **User:** founders and product managers.
- **Input:** market, region, and target customer.
- **Output:** competitor map, positioning, pricing evidence, recent product signals, and strategic
  gaps.
- **Why it is strong:** it produces an immediately understandable business artifact.
- **Portfolio metric:** freshness of sources and percentage of claims with a dated citation.

### 3. Responsible AI Policy Navigator

- **User:** engineering and governance teams.
- **Input:** AI use case and jurisdictions.
- **Output:** a sourced comparison of obligations, risks, controls, and questions for counsel.
- **Why it is strong:** it showcases constrained research and careful uncertainty handling.
- **Portfolio metric:** official-source coverage and stale-source detection rate.

### 4. Personalized Learning Path Builder

- **User:** learners changing careers.
- **Input:** target role, current skills, time per week, and deadline.
- **Output:** a sourced learning roadmap, projects, checkpoints, and a quiz.
- **Why it is strong:** it is the closest evolution of the original Study Buddy notebook and can be
  built quickly.
- **Portfolio metric:** learner completion and quiz improvement over time.

### 5. Evidence-Based Product Comparison

- **User:** buyers comparing technical products.
- **Input:** products, budget, and weighted requirements.
- **Output:** a cited decision matrix with trade-offs and uncertainty.
- **Why it is strong:** it makes the reviewer and source-quality rules visible in the demo.
- **Portfolio metric:** unsupported-claim rate and agreement with manually verified specifications.

## Recommendation

Build **AI Research Paper Scout** as the flagship version. Keep SourceCraft as the reusable engine,
then publish the specialized paper-scout branch or release as proof that the architecture is
customizable. It aligns most closely with a GenAI CV and gives you meaningful evaluation metrics
beyond “the output looks good.”
